The problem

Generic templates don't cut it on a BC worksite.

US-based forms miss BC classification

BC uses a three-tier atmospheric hazard classification — Low, Moderate, High — under s.9.1 and s.9.25. Most templates online are built for OSHA, not WorkSafeBC.

Free WorkSafeBC samples are starting points

WorkSafeBC's own samples don't have atmospheric reading columns, per-hazard standby person requirements, or amendment signature blocks needed for a real entry.

Enterprise software is overkill

SafetyCulture and SafetyEvolution charge $25–$30 per user per month. For a crew doing one confined space entry a week, that's $100+/month for a permit you could have for $67 once.
